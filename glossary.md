# Fragrance Glossary

A reference guide to common fragrance terminology.

## Accord

A combination of fragrance materials designed to produce a recognizable scent impression.

## Attar

A concentrated perfume oil traditionally associated with Middle Eastern and South Asian perfumery.

## Base notes

The slower-developing notes that form the lasting foundation of a fragrance.

## Decant

A smaller quantity of fragrance transferred from an original bottle into another container.

## Dry-down

The later stage of a fragrance after the opening and middle notes have developed.

## Eau de Parfum

A commonly used fragrance concentration that is generally richer than Eau de Toilette. Exact concentration varies by brand.

## Extrait de Parfum

A highly concentrated fragrance format. Greater concentration does not always mean stronger projection.

## Gourmand

A fragrance style featuring edible or dessert-like impressions such as vanilla, caramel, coffee, or chocolate.

## Heart notes

The middle stage of a fragrance, also called middle notes.

## Longevity

The approximate length of time a fragrance remains detectable.

## Maceration

A term sometimes used for the period during which fragrance materials blend and mature during production.

Consumers also sometimes use the term informally when discussing how a fragrance changes after opening, although this is not the same as commercial manufacturing.

## Niche fragrance

A loosely used term generally referring to fragrance-focused or specialist perfume houses. There is no universal industry definition.

## Oud

A scent material or accord associated with agarwood. Oud profiles can range from sweet and woody to smoky, leathery, earthy, or animalic.

## Projection

The distance a fragrance appears to radiate from the wearer.

## Sillage

The scent trail left by a fragrance as someone moves.

## Tester

A bottle originally intended for retail display or sampling. Testers may use simpler packaging and may or may not include a cap.

## Top notes

The opening scent impressions usually detected immediately after application.
