# Fragrance Notes and Accords

Fragrance descriptions often use notes and accords to explain how a perfume smells.

These descriptions help users understand the general character of a fragrance, but they do not necessarily reveal the complete ingredient formula.

## Fragrance notes

A fragrance note is a descriptive scent impression.

Examples include:

- bergamot;
- rose;
- vanilla;
- oud;
- sandalwood;
- amber;
- musk;
- leather;
- coffee;
- saffron.

A listed note may come from a natural material, a synthetic aroma molecule, or a combination of materials.

## Fragrance accords

An accord is a combination of materials designed to create a recognizable scent effect.

Common accords include:

- amber;
- aquatic;
- aromatic;
- balsamic;
- citrus;
- floral;
- fruity;
- gourmand;
- green;
- leather;
- marine;
- musky;
- powdery;
- smoky;
- spicy;
- sweet;
- woody.

## Top, middle, and base notes

### Top notes

Top notes are usually noticed first.

Examples:

- bergamot;
- lemon;
- orange;
- mint;
- light spices.

They often evaporate relatively quickly.

### Middle notes

Middle notes become more noticeable after the opening.

Examples:

- rose;
- jasmine;
- lavender;
- cinnamon;
- cardamom;
- fruit accords.

They often form the main body of the fragrance.

### Base notes

Base notes are usually slower to develop and may remain for the longest period.

Examples:

- amber;
- vanilla;
- oud;
- musk;
- patchouli;
- sandalwood;
- leather.

## Common Middle Eastern fragrance materials

### Oud

Oud is associated with agarwood.

It may smell:

- woody;
- smoky;
- resinous;
- leathery;
- earthy;
- animalic;
- sweet.

Oud materials and oud-style accords can vary significantly.

### Amber

Amber in perfumery is usually an accord rather than fossilized amber.

It often combines materials such as:

- labdanum;
- vanilla;
- benzoin;
- resins;
- warm woods.

### Musk

Modern musk notes are usually created using synthetic aroma materials.

They may smell:

- clean;
- soft;
- powdery;
- warm;
- skin-like.

### Saffron

Saffron accords may appear:

- spicy;
- dry;
- leathery;
- metallic;
- warm.

### Rose

Rose is widely used in Middle Eastern perfumery.

It may appear:

- fresh;
- jammy;
- spicy;
- dark;
- powdery;
- green.

### Incense

Incense accords may smell:

- smoky;
- resinous;
- mineral;
- balsamic;
- woody.

### Vanilla

Vanilla can add:

- sweetness;
- creaminess;
- warmth;
- softness;
- gourmand character.

## Fragrance families

Common fragrance families include:

- floral;
- woody;
- amber;
- fresh;
- aromatic;
- chypre;
- leather;
- gourmand.

Modern perfumes often combine several families.

## Subjectivity

Two people may interpret the same fragrance differently.

Skin chemistry, environment, memory, and personal experience can all affect scent perception.
