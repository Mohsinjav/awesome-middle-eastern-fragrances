# Fragrance Retailer Evaluation Checklist

This checklist can help buyers evaluate an online fragrance retailer before purchasing.

It does not guarantee the quality or authenticity of any seller.

## Business information

Look for:

- a clear business name;
- a working contact page;
- customer service email or phone number;
- a physical or mailing address where appropriate;
- an About page;
- clear terms and conditions.

## Website security

Check that:

- the website uses HTTPS;
- checkout is secure;
- payment options are clearly stated;
- the domain name matches the business;
- the site does not redirect unexpectedly.

## Product information

A reliable product page should usually identify:

- brand;
- product name;
- concentration;
- bottle size;
- product condition;
- stock status;
- price;
- shipping limitations.

## Shipping policy

Review:

- processing times;
- shipping carriers;
- delivery estimates;
- tracking availability;
- domestic and international restrictions;
- damaged or lost package procedures.

## Return and refund policy

Check:

- return period;
- whether opened fragrances can be returned;
- restocking fees;
- return shipping responsibility;
- refund processing times;
- final-sale exclusions.

## Pricing

Discount pricing is common in the fragrance market.

However, unusually low prices may require additional research.

Compare:

- multiple retailers;
- bottle size;
- concentration;
- tester versus retail packaging;
- older versus newer packaging;
- shipping fees.

## Customer feedback

Review feedback from more than one platform.

Look for patterns involving:

- shipping speed;
- customer service;
- packaging;
- order accuracy;
- refund handling;
- product condition.

Do not rely only on ratings displayed on the retailer's own website.

## Product condition

Confirm whether the item is:

- sealed retail packaging;
- a tester;
- unboxed;
- open-box;
- a sample;
- a decant;
- pre-owned.

## Authenticity claims

Be cautious with absolute claims.

Useful signs may include:

- transparent sourcing information;
- clear product photography;
- batch or production details when available;
- reasonable customer support;
- established operating history.

No single packaging detail or batch code should be treated as conclusive proof on its own.

## Warning signs

Possible concerns include:

- missing contact details;
- copied website content;
- unclear return terms;
- pressure to use irreversible payment methods;
- misleading product images;
- hidden fees;
- inconsistent business information;
- repeated unresolved customer complaints.
