# Fragrance Concentrations

Fragrance concentration refers to the approximate proportion of aromatic materials in a finished perfume product.

Exact percentages vary by brand and formula, so the ranges below are general guidelines.

## Common concentration types

| Concentration | General description | Typical character |
|---|---|---|
| Eau de Cologne | Light concentration | Fresh and short-wearing |
| Eau de Toilette | Light to moderate concentration | Airy and versatile |
| Eau de Parfum | Moderate to strong concentration | Fuller and often longer-lasting |
| Parfum | High concentration | Richer and usually more concentrated |
| Extrait de Parfum | Very high concentration | Dense and often close-wearing |
| Perfume Oil or Attar | Oil-based fragrance | Applied directly to skin |

## Eau de Cologne

Eau de Cologne is generally light and fresh.

It often emphasizes:

- citrus;
- herbs;
- aromatic notes;
- light florals.

It may require reapplication during the day.

## Eau de Toilette

Eau de Toilette is commonly used for everyday wear.

It often has:

- noticeable opening projection;
- lighter structure;
- moderate longevity;
- suitability for warm weather.

## Eau de Parfum

Eau de Parfum is one of the most common formats in modern perfumery.

It may offer:

- stronger body;
- greater depth;
- longer wear than lighter concentrations;
- more noticeable base notes.

Performance still depends on the individual formula.

## Parfum

Parfum usually contains a higher proportion of aromatic material.

It may feel:

- richer;
- smoother;
- denser;
- less sharp in the opening;
- closer to the skin.

## Extrait de Parfum

Extrait de Parfum is often highly concentrated.

It may have:

- strong depth;
- long wear;
- controlled projection;
- a heavier texture.

A stronger concentration does not always mean louder projection.

## Perfume oil and attar

Perfume oils and attars are usually alcohol-free or contain little alcohol.

They are commonly applied to:

- wrists;
- neck;
- behind the ears;
- pulse points.

They may project less than sprays but remain close to the skin for extended periods.

## What affects performance

Fragrance longevity and projection can be influenced by:

- skin type;
- temperature;
- humidity;
- clothing;
- number of sprays;
- formula;
- ingredient composition;
- storage conditions;
- personal scent perception.

## Important note

Concentration names are not fully standardized across every brand.

Always review the individual product description rather than assuming performance from concentration alone.
